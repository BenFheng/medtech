## 1. Cognitive Stack

### L-Theanine

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Caffeine (complementary focus + calm), Alpha-GPC (synergistic acetylcholine + GABA modulation) |
| **Inhibitors** | Stimulant medications (may reduce stimulant efficacy) |
| **Timing** | Any time of day; effects begin within 30-60 min, peak at 1-2 hours. 47-54% oral absorption. Can take with or without food. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Blood pressure meds** (may cause hypotension with captopril, losartan, amlodipine, etc.). **Sedatives** (additive drowsiness with diazepam, lorazepam, zolpidem). **Stimulants** (may reduce efficacy of ADHD medications). |

### Lion's Mane

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Bacopa Monnieri (complementary neuroprotection + neurogenesis). Effects build over weeks to months. |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors. |
| **Timing** | Morning for focus support. Can be taken with food. Effects build cumulatively over weeks-months of consistent use. |
| **Drug Interactions** | May interact with **antidepressants** affecting serotonin/dopamine. Caution with **sedatives** (may potentiate drowsiness). May influence **thyroid medications**. |

### Bacopa Monnieri

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Lion's Mane (complementary mechanisms). Fat enhances absorption of bacosides (fat-soluble). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors. |
| **Timing** | With meals (enhances absorption of fat-soluble bacosides, reduces GI discomfort). Evening dosing may support memory consolidation. Memory benefits noticeable in 4-6 weeks. |
| **Drug Interactions** | Caution with **antidepressants** (serotonergic effects). May potentiate **sedatives**. Possible interaction with **thyroid medications**. |

### Alpha-GPC

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | L-Theanine (smooth focus without jitters), Huperzine A (acetylcholinesterase inhibitor synergy), Phosphatidylserine (complementary phospholipid support). |
| **Inhibitors** | Anticholinergic drugs (opposing mechanisms). |
| **Timing** | 30-60 min before mental tasks or exercise for acute effects. Standard dosing: 400 mg three times daily with meals. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Acetylcholinesterase inhibitors** (donepezil, rivastigmine) - potentiates effects, may cause dizziness/nausea/muscle weakness; dose adjustment may be needed. **Scopolamine** and other anticholinergics (opposing effects). |

### Phosphatidylserine

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Omega-3 DHA (cell membrane support synergy), Alpha-GPC (complementary phospholipid). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors. |
| **Timing** | Morning or early afternoon for cortisol management. Effects begin within 1-2 weeks; optimal at 4-6 weeks. Take with food. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Blood thinners** (warfarin, heparin, aspirin, clopidogrel, ticlopidine) - may enhance anticoagulant effects. Avoid combining with **ginkgo, garlic, or vitamin E** unless supervised (additive blood-thinning). |

## 2. Sleep-Stress Stack

### Magnesium Bisglycinate

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Vitamin D3 (magnesium needed for D3 activation; D3 improves Mg absorption), Vitamin B6 (enhances intracellular Mg transport). |
| **Inhibitors** | High-dose calcium (competes for absorption). High-dose zinc at 142+ mg/day (competition at typical doses is modest). Phytates and oxalates in food. |
| **Timing** | Evening/before bed (promotes relaxation, supports sleep). Take with food to minimize GI discomfort. Glycinate form has superior absorption vs. oxide. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Antibiotics** (quinolones, tetracyclines - separate by 2+ hours). **Bisphosphonates** (separate by 2+ hours). **Blood pressure medications** (may potentiate hypotension). **Levothyroxine** (separate by 4+ hours). |

### Ashwagandha KSM-66

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Magnesium (complementary calming effects), Rhodiola Rosea (complementary adaptogenic support - different mechanisms). |
| **Inhibitors** | Caffeine/stimulants (may counteract calming effects). |
| **Timing** | Evening/before bed for sleep support, or split 300-500 mg twice daily with meals. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Thyroid medications** - may increase thyroid hormone levels; avoid or monitor closely. **Blood pressure medications** - risk of hypotension (dizziness, lightheadedness). **Immunosuppressants** - may stimulate immune system. **Sedatives/benzodiazepines** - additive drowsiness. **Diabetes medications** - may lower blood sugar. |

### Apigenin

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Magnesium bisglycinate (complementary GABA modulation), Glycine (complementary sleep mechanisms). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors. |
| **Timing** | 45-60 min before bed. Acts via GABA receptor modulation. Often consumed as chamomile extract. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Sedatives** (additive effects). **CYP enzyme substrates** (apigenin may inhibit certain CYP enzymes, affecting drug metabolism). |

### L-Tryptophan

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Vitamin B6 (cofactor for conversion to serotonin), Magnesium (supports enzymatic conversion), Carbohydrates (increase tryptophan transport across blood-brain barrier by reducing competing amino acids). |
| **Inhibitors** | High-protein meals (competing large neutral amino acids reduce brain uptake), Branched-chain amino acids (leucine, isoleucine, valine compete for the same transporter). |
| **Timing** | Evening, 30-60 min before bed. May take a few days to build serotonin levels; improvements in 1-2 weeks. Take on relatively empty stomach or with carbs (not high-protein). |
| **Drug Interactions** | **SSRIs/SNRIs/MAOIs** - SERIOUS RISK of serotonin syndrome. Do NOT combine. **5-HTP** - do not combine (same pathway, serotonin syndrome risk). **Sedatives** - additive drowsiness. |

### Glycine

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Magnesium (complementary sleep support), Apigenin (complementary GABA modulation). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors. |
| **Timing** | 60 min before bed. Effective dose: 3-5 g/day. Reduces sleep onset latency and next-day sleepiness. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Clozapine** (glycine may interact). **Antipsychotics** (may modulate NMDA receptor activity). Generally well-tolerated with minimal known drug interactions. |

## 3. Longevity Stack

### NMN (Nicotinamide Mononucleotide)

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Trans-Resveratrol (activates sirtuins that use NAD+; complementary pathways), CoQ10 (combined cardiovascular benefits shown stronger than either alone), PQQ (complementary mitochondrial biogenesis). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors. |
| **Timing** | Morning on empty stomach (aligns with natural NAD+ cycle). Sublingual absorption is slightly faster but oral is also effective via Slc12a8 transporter. If combining with fat-soluble resveratrol, take with a small fat-containing meal. |
| **Drug Interactions** | May interact with **glucose-regulating medications** (diabetes drugs). Consult provider if on prescription medications affecting energy metabolism or NAD+ pathways. |

### Trans-Resveratrol

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | **Piperine** (black pepper extract) - increases bioavailability by 229% (AUC) and max serum concentration by 1544% via inhibiting glucuronidation. **Quercetin** - inhibits ABC transporters, reducing sulphate metabolites and increasing free resveratrol. NMN (complementary sirtuin activation). |
| **Inhibitors** | Dietary fat (paradoxically, high-fat meals may reduce bioavailability despite resveratrol being lipophilic). Rapid hepatic metabolism is the main challenge. |
| **Timing** | Morning with NMN. Take with piperine for dramatically improved absorption. Low-to-moderate fat meal. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **CYP3A4 substrates** - resveratrol may inhibit CYP3A4 metabolism, affecting many drugs. **Blood thinners** - mild antiplatelet effects. **Estrogen-sensitive conditions** - has weak estrogenic activity. |

### CoQ10 (Ubiquinol)

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Omega-3 fatty acids (fish oil enhances CoQ10 absorption as a fat-soluble nutrient), PQQ (complementary mitochondrial support), Dietary fat (essential for absorption). |
| **Inhibitors** | None well-documented from nutrients; statins deplete CoQ10 production. |
| **Timing** | With a fat-containing meal (critical for absorption). Ubiquinol form is more bioavailable than ubiquinone. Typical dose: 200 mg once daily. No specific AM vs PM preference; consistency matters. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Warfarin** - may reduce anticoagulant efficacy. **Blood pressure medications** - may potentiate hypotensive effects. **Statins** - statins reduce CoQ10 synthesis; supplementation may be beneficial. **Insulin/diabetes drugs** - may affect blood sugar. |

### PQQ (Pyrroloquinoline Quinone)

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | CoQ10 (complementary mitochondrial support - PQQ promotes biogenesis, CoQ10 supports function), NMN (complementary NAD+ and mitochondrial pathways). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors. |
| **Timing** | Morning with breakfast or before lunch. Take with a meal containing some fat for optimal absorption. |
| **Drug Interactions** | Limited data on drug interactions. Consult provider if on medications. Generally considered safe at 10-20 mg/day. |

### Astaxanthin

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Omega-3 EPA/DHA (synergistic antioxidant effects at low concentrations; protective in cardiometabolic and neurodegenerative conditions), Dietary fat (essential - fat-soluble carotenoid). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors. |
| **Timing** | Immediately after a fat-containing meal (absorption and retention much higher with food vs. fasted). |
| **Drug Interactions** | May potentiate **blood pressure medications** (has mild hypotensive effects). May interact with **hormone therapies** (inhibits 5-alpha reductase). |

## 4. Skin-Radiance Stack

### Phytoceramides

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Hyaluronic Acid (complementary hydration mechanisms - ceramides seal moisture, HA attracts it), Vitamin C (improves absorption and effectiveness), Dietary fat (ceramides are lipids; fat improves absorption). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors. |
| **Timing** | Morning with a meal for optimal absorption. Once daily dosing. |
| **Drug Interactions** | Limited documented drug interactions. Generally well-tolerated. |

### Hyaluronic Acid (Oral)

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Vitamin C (improves effectiveness), Collagen Peptides (complementary joint and skin support), Phytoceramides (complementary moisture barrier). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors. |
| **Timing** | With food. Can be taken any time of day. |
| **Drug Interactions** | Minimal documented drug interactions for oral supplementation. |

### Vitamin C (Liposomal)

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Collagen Peptides (vitamin C is essential cofactor for collagen synthesis), Iron (vitamin C dramatically enhances non-heme iron absorption). |
| **Inhibitors** | High-dose vitamin C may interfere with **copper absorption**. May compete with **vitamin B12** for absorption at very high doses. |
| **Timing** | Empty stomach increases absorption. Liposomal form provides ~4x higher absorption than standard. Can split doses throughout the day. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Estrogen-containing contraceptives** (may increase estrogen levels). **Aluminum-containing antacids** (increases aluminum absorption). **Chemotherapy drugs** (antioxidant activity may theoretically reduce efficacy - controversial). **Statins/niacin** (may reduce effectiveness of statin-niacin combinations). |

### Collagen Peptides

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | **Vitamin C** (essential cofactor for collagen synthesis - maintains iron in active form within hydroxylation enzymes), Hyaluronic Acid (complementary skin/joint support). Note: Vitamin C does NOT enhance collagen peptide absorption, but is required for the body to synthesize collagen from absorbed peptides. |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors for absorption. |
| **Timing** | With food. For connective tissue: 30-60 min before tendon-loading exercise with vitamin C may support tissue adaptation. |
| **Drug Interactions** | Minimal documented drug interactions. Generally very well-tolerated. |

## 5. Foundation Stack

### Vitamin D3

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | **Vitamin K2 MK-7** (critical synergy - D3 promotes calcium absorption, K2 directs calcium to bones and away from arteries), **Magnesium** (required for D3 activation/metabolism), Dietary fat (fat-soluble; take with fats or oil-based formulation). |
| **Inhibitors** | Orlistat (reduces fat-soluble vitamin absorption). Cholestyramine. Mineral oil. |
| **Timing** | Morning or with largest meal containing fat. Consistency matters more than specific time. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Thiazide diuretics** (risk of hypercalcemia when combined). **Cardiac glycosides** (digoxin - increased calcium may worsen arrhythmias). **Corticosteroids** (reduce calcium absorption, may increase D3 need). **Phenytoin/phenobarbital** (increase D3 metabolism). |

### Vitamin K2 MK-7

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | **Vitamin D3** (synergistic for bone and cardiovascular health), Dietary fat (fat-soluble). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors. |
| **Timing** | With a fat-containing meal. Long half-life (~72 hours) means once-daily dosing is sufficient. Morning or evening does not significantly impact efficacy. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Warfarin and other vitamin K antagonists** - CRITICAL INTERACTION. K2 directly opposes warfarin's mechanism. Do NOT supplement without physician supervision if on anticoagulants. |

### Omega-3 EPA/DHA

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Astaxanthin (synergistic antioxidant protection of omega-3s from oxidation), CoQ10 (enhanced absorption; complementary cardiovascular support), Vitamin E (protects omega-3s from lipid peroxidation). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors for absorption. |
| **Timing** | With a fat-containing meal for best absorption. Can be taken AM or PM. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Blood thinners** (warfarin, aspirin, clopidogrel) - mild bleeding risk at doses >3g/day; generally safe under 3g/day even with antiplatelets. **Blood pressure medications** (may potentiate hypotensive effects). |

### Zinc Picolinate

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Vitamin C (modest enhancement of zinc absorption), Animal protein (enhances absorption). |
| **Inhibitors** | **Copper** (high zinc induces metallothionein which sequesters copper - supplement copper at 1-2 mg if taking zinc long-term). **Iron** (competes for DMT1 transporter). **Calcium** (competes for absorption). **Phytates** (in whole grains, legumes). **Selenium** (antagonistic at certain doses). |
| **Timing** | Earlier in the day, ideally with food to reduce nausea. Separate from iron and calcium by 2+ hours. Zinc picolinate has ~43% better bioavailability than zinc gluconate. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Quinolone antibiotics** (ciprofloxacin - separate by 2h before or 4-6h after). **Tetracycline antibiotics** (separate by 2h before or 4-6h after). **Penicillamine** (separate by 1+ hour). **Cephalexin** (take zinc 3h after). 158 total known drug interactions. |

### B-Complex (Methylated)

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Magnesium (B6 enhances Mg transport; Mg supports B vitamin function), Iron (B12 and folate support iron utilization). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors at standard doses. |
| **Timing** | Morning with food (B vitamins can be energizing and may interfere with sleep if taken late). |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Metformin** (depletes B12; methylated B12 may be beneficial). **Proton pump inhibitors/H2 blockers** (reduce B12 absorption). **Levodopa** (B6 can reduce levodopa efficacy unless taken with carbidopa). **Phenytoin** (folate may reduce phenytoin levels). |

### Selenium

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Vitamin E (complementary antioxidant synergy), Iodine (both essential for thyroid function; selenium may protect against iodine-induced Hashimoto's). |
| **Inhibitors** | **High-dose zinc** (antagonistic effect on selenium absorption), Vitamin C at very high doses (may reduce selenite to elemental selenium, impairing absorption - take selenomethionine form to avoid). |
| **Timing** | With food. Can be taken any time of day. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Levothyroxine** - no direct interaction found, but separate by 4 hours from multivitamin/mineral combos as a precaution. **Cisplatin** and other chemotherapy drugs (selenium may be protective but consult oncologist). **Autoimmune conditions** - caution in MS, lupus, RA (may stimulate immune system). |

## 6. Performance Stack

### Rhodiola Rosea

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Cordyceps (complementary endurance support - take both 30 min before exercise), Ashwagandha (complementary adaptogenic stress resistance via different mechanisms). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors. |
| **Timing** | Morning or before exercise. Take on empty stomach 30 min before food for best absorption (though some individuals may experience GI discomfort). Dose: 250-600 mg. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **SSRIs/serotonergic drugs** - risk of mild serotonin syndrome (documented case with paroxetine). Can be combined with **tricyclic antidepressants** (lower risk). **CYP enzyme substrates** (may modulate drug metabolism). **Stimulants** (may potentiate stimulatory effects). |

### Creatine Monohydrate

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Carbohydrates (insulin spike enhances creatine uptake into muscle), Protein (complementary muscle support). |
| **Inhibitors** | **Caffeine** - mixed evidence; chronic co-supplementation may negate creatine's muscle relaxation time benefits. Some studies show no interference, others show caffeine counteracts creatine's effects. |
| **Timing** | Post-workout with carbohydrate source for maximum absorption. During loading phase: 20g/day split into 4 doses for 5-7 days. Maintenance: 3-5g/day. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Nephrotoxic drugs** (caution in those with kidney concerns). **NSAIDs** (theoretical additive kidney stress at high doses). Generally very safe with minimal drug interactions. |

### Cordyceps Militaris

| Category | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Enhancers** | Rhodiola Rosea (complementary endurance benefits), Ashwagandha (adaptogenic synergy). |
| **Inhibitors** | No well-documented nutrient inhibitors. |
| **Timing** | With meals (three times daily in clinical studies). Can take on empty stomach for enhanced absorption but food reduces GI discomfort. Best before exercise for performance benefits. Dose: 1-3 g/day. |
| **Drug Interactions** | **Anticoagulants/antiplatelets** (may enhance bleeding risk). **Immunosuppressants** (may counteract their effects). **Cyclosporine** (markedly decreases cyclosporine bioavailability). 72 moderate drug interactions documented. |

## 8. Master Antagonism Pairs

These combinations should be separated in timing or carefully managed:

| Pair | Issue | Recommendation |
|------|-------|---------------|
| **Zinc vs. Copper** | High zinc induces metallothionein that sequesters copper | Supplement 1-2 mg copper if taking >25 mg zinc long-term |
| **Zinc vs. Iron** | Compete for DMT1 transporter; mutual absorption inhibition | Separate by 2+ hours |
| **Calcium vs. Iron** | Calcium inhibits iron absorption via DMT1 competition | Separate by 2-4 hours |
| **Calcium vs. Magnesium** | High calcium impairs magnesium absorption | Separate high doses by 2+ hours |
| **Calcium vs. Zinc** | Compete for absorption pathways | Separate by 2+ hours |
| **Zinc vs. Selenium** | Antagonistic absorption at certain concentrations | Separate by 2+ hours if high doses |
| **High-protein meals vs. L-Tryptophan** | Competing amino acids reduce tryptophan brain uptake | Take tryptophan with carbs, not protein |
| **Caffeine vs. Creatine** | Chronic co-use may negate creatine's muscle relaxation benefits | Separate timing or accept trade-off |
| **Caffeine vs. Iron** | Caffeine reduces non-heme iron absorption | Separate by 1+ hour |
| **Stimulants vs. Ashwagandha** | Opposing mechanisms (stimulation vs. calming) | Take at different times of day |

## 10. Suggested Daily Timing Schedule

### Morning (with breakfast containing healthy fats)

| Supplement | Notes |
|------------|-------|
| Vitamin D3 + K2 MK-7 | Fat-soluble; take together with fat |
| Omega-3 EPA/DHA | Provides fat vehicle for other fat-soluble supplements |
| CoQ10 Ubiquinol | Requires fat for absorption; synergy with Omega-3 |
| Astaxanthin | Fat-soluble; take with fat-containing meal |
| PQQ | Best in morning with food |
| NMN | Morning aligns with NAD+ cycle (can take on empty stomach before breakfast, then eat) |
| Trans-Resveratrol + Piperine | Take with NMN; piperine dramatically improves absorption |
| B-Complex Methylated | Energizing; avoid PM dosing |
| Rhodiola Rosea | Stimulating; take AM on empty stomach or with breakfast |
| Phytoceramides | Morning with meal |
| Vitamin C Liposomal | Split dose: half AM |
| Collagen Peptides | Can take AM with vitamin C |

### Midday (with lunch)

| Supplement | Notes |
|------------|-------|
| Zinc Picolinate | Separate from morning calcium/iron sources by 2+ hours |
| Selenium | Take with food; separate from zinc if high doses |
| Alpha-GPC | For afternoon cognitive performance |
| L-Theanine | For calm focus; can pair with Alpha-GPC |
| Lion's Mane | Cognitive support through the day |
| Phosphatidylserine | AM or early PM for cortisol management |
| Cordyceps | If exercising in afternoon; otherwise with lunch |
| Creatine | Post-workout with carbs; or with lunch |
| Vitamin C Liposomal | Split dose: other half midday |
| Hyaluronic Acid | With food |

### Evening (with dinner or before bed)

| Supplement | Notes |
|------------|-------|
| Magnesium Bisglycinate | 60 min before bed; promotes relaxation/sleep |
| Glycine | 3-5 g, 60 min before bed |
| L-Tryptophan | 30-60 min before bed; take with carbs, NOT high protein |
| Apigenin | 45 min before bed; GABA receptor modulation |
| Ashwagandha KSM-66 | Evening for sleep support and cortisol reduction |
| Bacopa Monnieri | Evening for memory consolidation; take with food |

### Critical Separation Rules

1. **Zinc and Iron**: Separate by 2+ hours minimum
2. **Calcium and Iron**: Separate by 2-4 hours
3. **Calcium and Magnesium**: Separate high doses by 2+ hours
4. **Zinc and Calcium**: Separate by 2+ hours
5. **Antibiotics and minerals (Mg, Zn)**: Separate by 2 hours before or 4-6 hours after
6. **Levothyroxine and minerals/supplements**: Take thyroid medication 4+ hours before any supplements
7. **L-Tryptophan and SSRIs**: Do NOT combine
8. **Vitamin K2 and Warfarin**: Do NOT combine without physician supervision
9. **Caffeine and Creatine**: Separate timing if concerned about interference
